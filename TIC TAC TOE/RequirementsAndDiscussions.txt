We want to design TIC TAC TOE game play
1. Clarifying Questions & Answers
Candidate: Should the game allow different board sizes, like 4x4 or 5x5, or is it strictly 3x3?
Interviewer: For this discussion, let’s keep it simple and stick to a classic 3x3 board.

Candidate: What should happen if a player chooses an invalid cell (either out of bounds or already occupied)?
Interviewer: The move should be declined, and the system should ask the same player to choose a different valid cell. The turn does not switch.

Candidate: Do we need to keep track of a scoreboard across multiple games (wins, losses, draws)?
Interviewer: Yes, maintaining a scoreboard that tracks wins for 'X', wins for 'O', and draws across multiple sessions is required.

Candidate: Are we supporting a multiple teams?
Interviewer: No, let's strictly limit it to a 1 vs 1 local multiplayer mode. 


2. Requirements
Functional Requirements:

The game is played on a standard 3x3 grid.

There are two players playing against each other (Player vs. Player) taking turns.

Players use symbols 'X' and 'O'.

The system must detect a winning condition (horizontal, vertical, or diagonal) and announce the winner.

If the board is completely filled and no player has won, the system must declare a draw.

The system must reject invalid moves (e.g., playing out of bounds or on an already occupied cell) and prompt the same player to try again.

The system must maintain a scoreboard tracking the number of wins for each player and the total number of draws across multiple games.
